Wau

A Wau is a breathtaking Malaysian kite, famous for its intricate patterns and vibrant colors. It's a symbol of our nation's rich heritage and a beloved sight during festive celebrations. From its traditional bamboo and paper construction to its modern, innovative designs, the Wau continues to captivate hearts and skies.

Gasing

The Gasing is a traditional Malaysian spinning top, a symbol of strength, agility, and skill. Crafted from wood, it's spun with a flick of the wrist, competing for the longest spin time. This thrilling game has been enjoyed for generations, fostering camaraderie and competitive spirit among players.

Lompat Getah

Lompat Getah is a traditional Malaysian game of skill and coordination. Players jump over an elastic band stretched between two people, rhythmically chanting while increasing the band's height. It's a fun and energetic game that has been enjoyed by generations of Malaysians.

Congkak

Congkak is a traditional Malay board game requiring strategy and calculation. Players sow seeds (or stones) into pits and capture opponent's seeds. It's a popular pastime that sharpens mental agility and fosters friendly competition.

Batu Seremban

Batu Seremban is a traditional Malaysian game of skill and dexterity. Players toss small stones in various patterns, testing hand-eye coordination and agility. This engaging pastime has been enjoyed by generations of Malaysians, fostering both individual skill and competitive spirit.

Ketingting

Ketingting is a traditional Malaysian game that tests balance and agility. Players hop and jump through a pattern of squares drawn on the ground, tossing and retrieving a small object. It's a fun and active pastime that has been enjoyed by generations of children.

Sepak Takraw
